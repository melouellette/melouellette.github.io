About Me

I am currently completing the Geographic Information Systems - Application Specialist program at Fleming College, and I am working towards completing my Bachelor of Science in Biology at Trent University part-time. In 2019, I graduated from the Ecosystem Management Technologist - Advanced Diploma program also offered at Fleming College.

My past work history has focussed mainly on terrestrial ecology and outreach. My most recent position as a Resource Management Technician for Parks Canada, allowed me to familiarize myself with Federal Recovery Strategies and policies for Species at Risk. Using available data records and ArcGIS, I updated the Action Plans for 85 Species at Risk within Trent Severn Waterway watershed. I also gained valuable work experience at Conservation Halton as the Terrestrial Monitoring and Outreach Assistant where I assisted in ecological field work and data collection in support of the Long-term Environmental Monitoring Program and Species at Risk recovery projects. This included such tasks as ecological inventories and sampling, and forest health monitoring. I also assisted stewardship staff with the delivery of outreach events, workshops and activities related to habitat creation and restoration.

Before returning to school, I spent six years working for West 49 as the Assistant Store Manager, where I assisted with coaching, training, and motivating staff. This required excellent communication and teamwork through cooperation and adaptability to reach a common goal.

I have also volunteered at various organizations, including Oak Ridges Moraine Land Trust, Petawawa Research Forest, and Otonabeee Region Conservation Authority. I successfully completed a Baseline Documentation Report for a Conservation Easement Agreement on behalf of Oak Ridges Moraine Land Trust. I performed a full Ecological Land Classification and created maps of the property including invasive species, previous violations, and the final ELC polygons.

I possess excellent data management skills for the purposes of recording data collected in the field. I have working knowledge of software programs such as Microsoft Office, Collector and ArcGIS for purposes such as entering and organizing data, compiling reports, and preparing presentations.
